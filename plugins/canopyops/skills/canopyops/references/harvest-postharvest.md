# Harvest and Postharvest

Harvest readiness, drying readiness, quality disposition, and batch release are separate decisions.

## Harvest is a triangulation

Use the facility’s defined maturity criteria, representative sampling, crop history, plant health, intended product/quality profile, schedule, and risk. Visual gland or pistil observations may contribute but do not alone establish readiness. Record sampling locations and variation across the room.

## Drying is a product-zone process

Room averages can conceal cold, humid, stagnant, overloaded, or over-dried zones. Preserve product-zone temperature/RH, airflow state, load arrangement, time, mass change or validated moisture method, and deviations. Avoid abrupt unapproved changes that create surface/internal gradients or destroy diagnostic history.

**Moisture content** is total water by mass under a defined method. **Water activity** reflects the energy/availability of water under a defined method and relates more directly to microbial potential. They are not substitutes. Neither should be invented from RH or touch.

Any numeric acceptance threshold must come from the current facility specification, validated process, laboratory method, and applicable rule. Source-corpus example ranges are not release criteria.

## Holds and release

Suspected contamination, mold, abnormal odor, untraceable material, unsafe handling, or specification excursion requires a hold/incident path appropriate to facility authority. The agent can assemble evidence and a recommendation; only the accountable role can authorize harvest, disposition, or batch release.
