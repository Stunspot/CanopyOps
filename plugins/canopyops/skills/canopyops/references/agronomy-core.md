# Agronomy Core

Cultivation decisions are **plant × environment × root zone × operations × time**. A symptom or target stripped from that system is easy to recognize and easy to misapply.

## Read the crop as a changing system

- **Stage changes meaning.** Propagation, establishment, vegetative growth, transition, flowering, maturation, harvest, and drying have different demands and failure costs.
- **Rate matters with state.** A plausible temperature or feed value can still fail when the transition is too abrupt, the duration too long, or another system cannot carry the resulting load.
- **Pattern outranks resemblance.** Timing, distribution, progression, affected/unaffected comparisons, and recent change usually discriminate better than one leaf image.
- **Genotype is conditional.** Cultivar labels do not guarantee identity or stable performance. Record source, lot/clone lineage, health status, and facility observations.
- **Operations are causal.** Labor timing, sanitation, calibration, handoffs, maintenance, and record latency can create agronomic phenomena that look biological.

## Plan from constraints

Begin with actual room capacity: lighting distribution, sensible and latent cooling, dehumidification across lights-on/off transitions, airflow, irrigation delivery and drainage, electrical and water safety, monitoring, labor, and legal constraints. Targets are proposals until those dependencies can support them.

Use the facility profile as the canonical constraints record and the crop profile as the canonical biological/observational record. A crop plan joins them for one cycle; it does not overwrite either.

## Change discipline

Prefer one bounded, reversible intervention with a predicted response and monitoring window. Simultaneous changes may be necessary in an emergency, but they weaken attribution. Record what changed, when, why, who approved it, and what would trigger rollback or escalation.

## Evidence status

Direct measurement is not automatically good evidence: method, calibration, location, sampling, units, timing, and representativeness determine what it supports. A model interpretation remains an interpretation until discriminating evidence or qualified confirmation earns stronger status.
