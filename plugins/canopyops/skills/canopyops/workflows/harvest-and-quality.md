# Harvest and Quality

Use for harvest-window assessment, dry-room planning, drying deviations, quality protection, and release preparation.

Read `references/harvest-postharvest.md`, `references/safety-operations.md`, and the active crop profile. Use `assets/harvest-readiness.md` and `assets/drying-log.csv`; open an incident record for excursions or contamination concerns.

1. Define the decision separately: harvest timing, cut authorization, drying intervention, quality disposition, or batch release. These have different evidence and authority.
2. Triangulate readiness from the facility’s approved criteria, crop stage and history, representative sampling, plant condition, production schedule, and intended quality profile. Do not reduce readiness to one visual cue.
3. For drying, align time, product-zone temperature/RH, airflow condition, load distribution, mass-loss observations, moisture method, and water-activity results where available. Distinguish moisture content from water activity.
4. Treat mold suspicion, contaminated product, abnormal odor, unsafe equipment, or specification excursion as an incident. Preserve evidence and prevent uncontrolled movement while accountable humans determine disposition.
5. Produce a readiness brief with evidence, uncertainty, recommendation, hold points, owner, and verification condition. The agent may prepare release evidence; it cannot release the batch.

No example threshold in package doctrine supersedes the facility specification, validated process, laboratory method, or current jurisdiction rule.
