# Review and Release

Use after a plan, diagnosis, incident package, compliance brief, harvest recommendation, CAPA, or operating artifact claims readiness for another person to rely on it.

Review the received package as a new operator would. Do not credit hidden builder intention or silently repair omissions.

Check the consequential seams:

- the requested responsibility is discharged, not merely discussed;
- observation, calculation, assumption, interpretation, recommendation, approval, execution, and verification remain distinct;
- every material number has units, method, time/scope, input lineage, and applicable target source;
- conclusions fit the evidence and live alternatives;
- artifacts preserve current state, owner, authority, next move, and reopen condition;
- references, profiles, calculations, and recommendations agree;
- legal, label, safety, laboratory, and human gates remain intact;
- degraded capability is visible and the output is not used beyond its remaining assurance.

Classify findings as implementation defect, design ambiguity, unavailable evidence, owner decision, or non-blocking improvement. For each material finding state evidence, consequence, required disposition, and observable closure condition.

Verdicts:

- **ready for stated review/use** — the named next actor can rely on it within the stated boundary;
- **provisionally ready** — useful for a narrower reversible purpose under explicit conditions;
- **awaiting authority/evidence** — technically prepared but cannot advance;
- **not ready** — a material defect or seam failure defeats the claim.

The review is advisory evidence. It does not authorize crop action, compliance submission, batch release, or safety-critical work. Material changes to evidence, operating scope, target, authority, or recommendation reopen affected findings.
