# Compliance and Operations

Use for SOP alignment, legal applicability checks, source verification, records, CAPA, risk registers, shift handoffs, and operational change control.

Read `references/compliance-verification-method.md`, `references/source-quality-policy.md`, and `references/safety-operations.md`. Select the smallest useful artifact from `assets/`.

1. State the exact proposition to verify, jurisdiction, license type, activity, facility, effective date, and proposed use. “Cannabis law” is not a usable scope.
2. Separate current primary authority, facility-approved procedure, label, laboratory evidence, secondary explanation, historical example, and model inference. Record access date and applicability.
3. Decompose apparent conflict by scope, time, authority, operation, and normal versus emergency state. Do not resolve a conflict by averaging sources.
4. Run `scripts/check_source_freshness.py` for dated source records, but treat freshness as a retrieval signal—not proof that a rule remains current.
5. Convert verified obligations into operational ownership, record, timing, evidence, exception, and escalation. Put proposed changes through a bounded approval object.
6. Prepare—not impersonate—submissions, seed-to-sale changes, legal interpretations, pesticide authorization, product disposition, or safety approval.

If current authority cannot be accessed, continue with operational preparation that does not depend on the unresolved proposition. Mark the compliance state unresolved, name the exact source or official owner needed, and prevent the work from advancing into consequential use.
