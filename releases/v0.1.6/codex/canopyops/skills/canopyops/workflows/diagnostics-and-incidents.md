# Diagnostics and Incidents

Use for abnormal crop appearance, environmental excursions, pest/pathogen concerns, quality deviations, and unexplained performance change.

Read `references/diagnostic-field-guide.md`, then the domain references implicated by the evidence. Open `assets/incident-report.md`; use `assets/crop-walk.csv`, `assets/risk-register.csv`, and `assets/capa-record.md` as the episode develops.

1. Stabilize the phenomenon before naming a cause: onset, progression, distribution, affected/unaffected comparisons, stage, recent changes, measurements, method, sensor location, images, and baseline.
2. Test the observation process. Check calibration, placement, sampling, unit, timestamps, aggregation, and whether an intervention has already altered the evidence.
3. Maintain a compact differential of materially different mechanisms. For each live explanation record support, contradiction, expected companion signs, discriminating evidence, and consequence of missing it.
4. Separate **containment**, **diagnostic action**, and **corrective action**. Prefer reversible containment safe across the live differential; preserve samples and records before interventions erase evidence.
5. Escalate suspected regulated pests/pathogens, pesticide decisions, worker hazards, product contamination, or substantial crop disposition to the named human authority. Images and pattern recognition do not confirm a pathogen, residue, or compliance state.
6. Update the incident record as evidence changes. Close only when remaining uncertainty no longer changes treatment, qualified confirmation is obtained, or the matter transfers with a clear next move.

Never convert resemblance into certainty, nutrient symptoms into an automatic feed increase, or a tidy narrative into a single theatrical root cause.
