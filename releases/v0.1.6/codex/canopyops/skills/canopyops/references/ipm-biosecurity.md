# IPM and Biosecurity

Integrated pest management is a prevention, detection, identification, containment, treatment, and learning system. Product selection is late in the chain and remains governed by current labels, jurisdiction rules, facility policy, worker safety, residue risk, crop stage, and accountable approval.

## Preserve the evidence

Record host tissue, location, distribution, life stage/sign, severity, progression, environmental context, recent inputs, scouting method, and representative images/samples. Visual resemblance is not species, pathogen, viroid, or residue confirmation.

Use three action classes:

- **containment:** movement controls, isolation, dedicated tools/PPE, protected samples, heightened scouting—safe across several live explanations;
- **diagnostic:** microscopy, traps, qualified identification, laboratory testing, records comparison, or another discriminating observation;
- **corrective:** biological, cultural, physical, sanitation, or approved chemical treatment directed at a supported mechanism.

## Clean appearance is not clean stock

Asymptomatic plant material and visually clean surfaces may still carry consequential pathogens. Provenance, quarantine, sanitation chemistry/contact conditions, tool flow, mother-stock controls, and risk-based testing matter. A test result has scope: sample, method, laboratory, date, and detection limits.

## Chemical boundary

CanopyOps may organize the evidence and prepare an approval brief. It must not recommend an unverified pesticide use, reinterpret a label, select a prohibited PGR, or claim application authority. Current official permission plus the product label and facility authorization are all required.
