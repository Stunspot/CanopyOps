# Source Provenance

## Canonical build sources

- Approved design: `design-record/canopyops-cannabis-farming-augment-design-spec.md` in the development prototype.
- Operative persona: `persona-cannabis-farmer-ella-greenfield-v2.md`, copied byte-for-byte to `personas/ella-greenfield-v2.md`.
- Source corpus: `knowledge-cannabis-horticulture-knowledge-base.md` in the development prototype (123 cited items in the supplied corpus).

The source corpus is an owner-directed Gemini Deep Research artifact created for Collaborative Dynamics from owner-supplied research direction and materials. It remains build evidence rather than shipped doctrine. CanopyOps claims no ownership over third-party publications, titles, facts, or linked sources represented in that research; their respective rights and authority remain with their originators.

The raw knowledge base remains outside the runtime package because it mixes durable horticultural relationships, vendor/secondary explanations, research, historical/current-looking regulatory examples, and claims whose scope or currency must not be flattened into doctrine.

## Distillation policy

Runtime references preserve the corpus’s useful causal structures and distinctions while downgrading generic targets and volatile legal claims to verification inputs. They do not claim that every source-corpus citation was independently checked during package construction.

Consequential current verification should prioritize primary sources such as issuing regulators, statutes/regulations, official labels, facility-approved SOPs, qualified laboratory methods, extension literature, and peer-reviewed research within scope. Secondary and vendor sources may locate or explain authority but do not replace it.

Notable primary/technical source families represented in the corpus include Illinois official regulations and agency materials, USDA/extension substrate and tissue-culture resources, Purdue/Cornell/UMass horticultural methods, and peer-reviewed work on greenhouse pathogens, lighting/leaf temperature, substrate hydraulics, crop steering, and postharvest behavior. Consult the development corpus for the complete URL list and captured claims.

## Update boundary

Before release maintenance, re-check any runtime claim that acquires a precise threshold, product permission, jurisdiction requirement, legal interpretation, laboratory action limit, equipment specification, or dated “current” status. Record changes in the compliance verification artifact and changelog.
