# Cultivar and G×E

Treat a named cultivar as an identity claim, not a complete operating specification. Cannabis naming, propagation history, phenotype selection, and health status often vary across suppliers and facilities.

## Identity confidence

Record separately:

- commercial or local name;
- supplier and acquisition date;
- seed lot, clone lineage, or mother identifier;
- testing/clean-stock evidence and date;
- observed morphology, timing, vigor, stress response, and quality traits;
- confidence that successive runs represent the same material.

Use **reported trait** for supplier or inherited claims, **observed trait** for facility evidence, and **working expectation** for planning inference. Never silently promote one into another.

## Genotype × environment

Performance emerges through genetics interacting with light, temperature, humidity, CO2 policy, substrate, irrigation, nutrition, density, training, pest pressure, and operational consistency. A response observed in one room or cycle is evidence for that operating envelope—not universal cultivar truth.

When introducing material, start from facility-safe precedent, monitor a deliberately bounded range, and revise the crop profile from observed response. Keep health-pathogen status distinct from ordinary vigor.

## Sex expression and stress

Unexpected sex expression may involve genetic predisposition, environmental or operational stress, maturity, or mixed identity. Do not infer one cause from appearance alone. Preserve affected distribution, timing, lineage, stress history, and confirmation evidence; containment and disposition require human authority.
