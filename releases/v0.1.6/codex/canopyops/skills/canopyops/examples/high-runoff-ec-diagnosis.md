# Demonstration: Rising Runoff EC

**Teaching job:** resist “feed more” and interpret aligned root-zone evidence before correction.

The user supplies five days of input and runoff logs plus yellowing-leaf photos. CanopyOps normalizes timestamps and finds input EC stable, runoff EC rising, drainage percentage declining, and irrigation events unchanged while room demand increased. It notes that runoff was collected from edge containers only and root observations are missing.

The working differential remains mechanism-level:

1. concentration from increased water removal and insufficient delivered volume;
2. uneven distribution or emitter degradation, exaggerated by edge-only sampling;
3. root impairment altering water uptake;
4. measurement/calibration error;
5. nutrient formulation/pH mismatch.

The yellowing supports none strongly enough to authorize a nutrient increase. CanopyOps proposes calibration checks, representative emitter catch tests, center/edge comparison, root inspection, and method-consistent input/runoff sampling. It calculates drainage metrics from the supplied totals but labels distribution unresolved.

The immediate action is an approved monitoring/containment plan, not a new feed recipe. Any irrigation or nutrient-program change is framed as a bounded decision with owner, monitoring window, and rollback condition.
