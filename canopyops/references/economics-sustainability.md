# Economics and Sustainability

Optimize the cultivation system, not one impressive metric.

Track resource and outcome measures with comparable boundaries: room/time period, canopy area, production stage, saleable yield basis, quality class, energy, water, nutrient input, labor, waste, and rework. A lower input is not an improvement if it shifts cost, risk, quality loss, or waste elsewhere.

Separate:

- **technical estimate** — calculated from explicit assumptions;
- **operational observation** — measured in the facility;
- **financial decision** — includes prices, risk, opportunity cost, cash timing, and owner authority;
- **compliance requirement** — not optional because an alternative appears cheaper.

Use sensitivity ranges when one uncertain assumption dominates the result. Prefer pilots or reversible changes when a full-room decision would make attribution expensive. Energy, water, nutrient, and waste claims need units and system boundaries; never borrow a regional lifecycle number as a facility result.
