# Diagnostic Field Guide

## Stabilize the phenomenon

Capture onset, trajectory, room/zone pattern, cultivar/lineage, stage, affected organ and age, upper/lower canopy, root/substrate observations, affected/unaffected controls, recent changes, environment/irrigation records, scouting, and measurement quality.

## Build mechanism-level alternatives

Use only live explanations that change evidence or treatment. Common groups include:

| Mechanism | Distinguishing work |
|---|---|
| measurement or sampling artifact | calibration, placement, method replication, raw versus aggregated logs |
| environment/airflow/light | spatial and transition pattern, leaf temperature, canopy position, equipment state |
| root-zone water/oxygen | dryback/distribution, roots, container pattern, irrigation timing |
| ion concentration/pH/uptake | aligned input, extract/runoff trends, method consistency, roots and demand |
| pest/pathogen/viroid | scouting signs, distribution/lineage, qualified ID or laboratory evidence |
| genetics/development | lineage pattern, stage timing, repeat runs, stress interaction |
| operational/chemical event | work orders, applications, sanitation, mixing, labor and equipment changes |

For each alternative: what it explains; what it does not; expected companion evidence; best obtainable discriminator; safe containment; cause-specific action withheld.

## Close at the decision threshold

Do not investigate forever. Close when one explanation supports the next reversible action, remaining alternatives no longer change treatment, or qualified confirmation/transfer occurs. An unresolved close must preserve leading alternatives, safe action, evidence most likely to change the view, and the reopen/escalation condition.
