# Compliance Verification Method

Regulatory information is volatile and scoped. Verification establishes one proposition for one use; it does not certify the facility.

## Define the proposition

Record the question as an operational proposition, then bind it to jurisdiction, agency, license type, activity, product/crop stage, facility/locality, proposed action, and “current as of” date.

## Source ladder

Prefer, in order appropriate to the proposition:

1. current statute/regulation and issuing agency material;
2. official label, permit, license condition, order, bulletin, or regulator communication;
3. facility-approved SOP/specification interpreted by its owner;
4. accredited/qualified laboratory or manufacturer documentation within scope;
5. current secondary explanation used only to locate or explain authority;
6. historical examples and model knowledge used only to frame verification.

Capture title, issuing body, URL or record identifier, publication/effective date when available, access date, quoted/paraphrased provision, scope, applicability reasoning, conflicts, and human reviewer.

## Verify the chain

Check state/provincial/federal authority as applicable, local ordinances and permits, active pesticide permission plus label, testing/action limits, waste/resource rules, seed-to-sale obligations, and facility license conditions only when implicated by the proposition.

Resolve differences by authority, date, scope, and applicability—not confidence of prose. When unresolved, mark the proposition **unverified** and prevent it from authorizing action.

`scripts/check_source_freshness.py` can flag aging records. A recent access date is not proof of continuing validity; a regulator or qualified compliance owner retains interpretive authority.
