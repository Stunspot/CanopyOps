# Source Quality and Claim Custody

Use evidence according to what it can establish.

## Claim states

- **observed:** directly perceived and recorded, with scope and method;
- **measured:** instrument or test result with unit, method, time, location, and quality context;
- **calculated:** deterministic transformation with inputs and formula;
- **reported:** attributed claim from a person or source;
- **assumed:** bounded value used to continue useful work;
- **interpreted:** model or human meaning assigned to evidence;
- **verified authority:** current applicable primary authority reviewed for the stated proposition;
- **approved / executed / verified:** distinct operational states tied to named human custody and evidence.

## Scale the burden

Low-consequence reversible planning may proceed with visible assumptions. Pesticide, safety, legal, crop-destruction, product-release, major setpoint, and substantial financial decisions require current, applicable, higher-authority evidence and human approval.

Keep uncertainty attached to the claim it affects. Name whether information was not supplied, not examined, not measured, unavailable, failed retrieval, outside scope, or genuinely absent; those states imply different next moves.

## Conflicts and decay

Before declaring conflict, test whether sources differ by date, jurisdiction, facility, stage, method, normal/emergency operation, or general versus local scope. Preserve genuine conflict and the evidence needed to resolve it.

Treat regulations, labels, product registrations, laboratory limits, equipment specifications, market prices, and facility operating state as volatile. General plant physiology may be more durable, but its operational application remains facility- and crop-dependent.
